# Product Vision — Treehouse (Personal Website)

## One-liner

A fast, credible personal website that presents Sk. Farhad Uddin Ahmed as a
Lead Software Engineer and AI expert — and converts the right visitors into a
conversation.

## Who it's for

1. **Recruiters / hiring managers** evaluating senior/lead engineering & AI roles.
2. **Potential clients / collaborators** looking for AI/backend consulting.
3. **Peers & the technical community** who arrive from GitHub, LinkedIn, or X.

The audience is technical or technical-adjacent. Some of them WILL look at the
source and the repo. The site itself is part of the portfolio — it should be
clean under the hood, not just on the surface.

## What "good" looks like

- **Credible.** No template author's name in the metadata, no dead CDNs, no
  lorem ipsum. Everything on the page is real and current.
- **Fast & low-maintenance.** Static content, minimal dependencies, cheap to
  host, nothing to patch or secure beyond the essentials.
- **Professional & safe.** Business contact only — no home address or personal
  phone number exposed to the public internet.
- **Discoverable.** Proper title tags, meta description, and Open Graph so links
  shared on LinkedIn/X render well and the site ranks for his name.

## Core content (the whole product)

- **Home** — who he is, a short bio, services offered, recent notable work.
- **About** — experience, technical skills, education, publications.
- **Contact** — a form + a professional email. That's it.
- **Resume** — a link to a current PDF.

## Explicit non-goals

- Not a CMS. He edits content by committing a file, not through an admin panel.
- Not a database-backed app. The content is small and changes a few times a year.
- Not a blog — unless he actually starts writing (see roadmap). No dead
  "coming soon" pages.
- No user accounts, no auth surface, no comment system.

## Guiding principle

**Least maintenance for maximum credibility.** Every feature must earn its
upkeep. When in doubt, delete it.
